“Peace at Home, Peace in the World” – Mustafa Kemal Atatürk

Mustafa Kemal Atatürk was born in 1881 in Thessaloniki, and completed his military education during the final years of the Ottoman Empire. He achieved his greatest military victory in 1915 during the Gallipoli Campaign, and in 1919, he launched the Turkish War of Independence by arriving in Samsun, leading the resistance against foreign occupation forces. On October 29, 1923, he founded the Republic of Turkey and became its first president. After the proclamation of the Republic, Atatürk implemented numerous radical reforms to modernize Turkey. He introduced changes in secularism, education, law, women's rights, and the economy, transforming Turkey into a modern state. Atatürk passed away on November 10, 1938, but he remains regarded as Turkey’s most significant leader, and his principles continue to guide the country’s governance and development.
